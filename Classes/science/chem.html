About The Course

AP Chemistry is an introductory college-level chemistry course. Students cultivate their understanding of chemistry through inquiry-based lab investigations as they explore the four Big Ideas: scale, proportion, and quantity; structure and properties of substances; transformations; and energy.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Monday, May 1st 12:00 PM

• Multiple Choice: 60 Questions | 1 Hour 30 minutes | 50% of Exam Score

• Free Response: 7 Questions | 1 Hour 45 Minutes | 50% of Exam Score

• Long Answer: 3 questions (10 pt)

• Short Answer: 4 questions (4 pt)

• The questions assess all 6 of the course skills: models and representations, question and method, representing data and phenomena, model analysis, mathematical routines, and argumentation.

Resources

• Study Tips

• Use flashcards to drill the details. AP Chemistry involves a lot of memorization of facts, formulas, definitions, and concepts. Flashcards are a great way to review these details and test your recall. You can make your own flashcards using paper cards or an online tool like Quizlet

• Study example questions and practice exams. One of the best ways to prepare for the AP Chemistry exam is to practice with real or realistic questions that mimic the format and difficulty of the exam. You can find official practice questions and exams on the College Board website or in review books. You can also use online resources like Albert.io or Fiveable to access more practice questions and quizzes by topic area.
