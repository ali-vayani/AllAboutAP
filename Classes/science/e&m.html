AP Physics C: Electricity and Magnetism

About The Course

AP Physics C: Electricity and Magnetism is a one-semester, calculus-based, college-level physics course, especially appropriate for students planning to specialize or major in one of the physical sciences or engineering. Students cultivate their understanding of physics through classroom study and activities as well as hands-on laboratory work as they explore concepts like change, force interactions, fields, and conservation.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Tuesday, May 9th 2:00 PM

• Multiple Choice: 35 Questions | 45 Minutes | 50% of the total score.

• 5 Answer choices per question

• Free Response: 3 Questions | 45 Minutes | 50% of the total score.

• The FRQs will assess all 7 science practices: visual representations, question and method, representing data and phenomena, data analysis, theoretical relationships, mathematical routines, and argumentation.

• 15 points each.

• One will include an experimental or lab component.

Resources

• Study Tips

• Work through as many practice problems as you can, especially from past AP exams or official College Board materials. This will help you familiarize yourself with the format and difficulty of the questions, and test your understanding of the concepts

• Review the calculus concepts that are relevant to electromagnetism, such as derivatives, integrals, vector calculus, etc. The actual calculus on the AP exam is rather light, but it helps to understand how it applies to physics
