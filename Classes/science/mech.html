AP Physics C: Mechanics

About The Course

AP Physics C Mechanics has a high pass rate at 74%. This means that almost three-quarters of students who take this exam pass it. Despite its pass rate, AP Physics C Mechanics is a very challenging class. You’re expected to have a strong Physics foundation along with knowledge of Calculus. You should expect to be met with concepts such as forces, particle interactions, gravitational fields, and conservation.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Tuesday, May 9th 2:00 PM

• Multiple Choice: 35 Questions | 45 Minutes | 50% of the total score.

• 5 Answer choices per question

• Free Response: 3 Questions | 45 Minutes | 50% of the total score.

• The FRQs will assess all 7 science practices: visual representations, question and method, representing data and phenomena, data analysis, theoretical relationships, mathematical routines, and argumentation.

• 15 points each.

• One will include an experimental or lab component.

Resources

• Study Tips

• Physics is all about problem-solving, so when approaching a problem, break it down into its fundamental principles, identify the key concepts, and apply them systematically to reach the solution.

• Work through as many practice problems as you can, especially from past AP exams or official College Board materials. This will help you familiarize yourself with the format and difficulty of the questions, and test your understanding of the concepts
