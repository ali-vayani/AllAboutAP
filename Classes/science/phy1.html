About The Course

AP Physics 1 is notorious for having the lowest pass rate out of any AP Exam along with being very difficult. Despite this, it is one of the more popular AP Classes with over 130,00 students taking it in the 2021 school year. AP Physics 1 is an algebra-based, introductory college-level course. You can expect to learn Physics that’s on the more Mechanical side, such as Kinematics, Momentum, Forces, and more. You should also expect to participate in laboratory work and work hands-on during this class.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Thursday, May 11th 12:00 PM

• Multiple Choice: 50 Questions | 1 hour 30 mins | 50% of total score

• 5 out of the 50 questions are multi-select -- meaning 2 answer choices are correct.

• Free Response: 5 questions | 1 hour 30 minutes | 50% of your total score.

• Experimental Design (12pt)

• You’re given a scenario where you’re asked to find out if the hypothesis is true or not through an experiment. You’re required to provide in-depth steps that can be done in a classroom environment.

• Qualitative/Quantitative Translation (12pt)

• These are a series of questions that require answers that go between word explanation and mathematical explanations.

• Paragraph Argument (7 pt)

• As the name states, the paragraph argument requires you to answer questions in a well-written paragraph. Your answers can contain drawings and equations to explain your reasoning.

• 2 Short Answer Questions (7 pts each)

• These are a set of questions that can have multiple parts.
