About The Course

AP Statistics is an introductory college-level statistics course that is centered around collecting, analyzing, and drawing conclusions from data. Students cultivate a strong understanding of how to use technology, critical thinking and investigations to make data-based conclusions and predictions. While it is classified as a math course; it is very conceptual-based, containing strong amounts of explanation and writing.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Thursday, May 4th 12:00 PM

• Multiple Choice: 40 Questions | 1 hour 30 Minutes | 50% of Exam Score

• Includes individual questions or sets of questions based on shared prompts

• Free Response: 6 Questions | 1 hour 30 Minutes | 50% of Exam Score

• Part A: 5 Short Answer Questions (Suggested time: ~60 mins | 12 mins each)

• 1 multipart question on collecting data

• 1 multipart question on exploring data

• 1 multipart question on probability and sampling distributions

• 1 question on inference

• 1 question that combines 2+ skill categories

• Part B: 1 Investigative Task (Suggested time: ~30 mins)

• 1 investigative task that tests several skill categories and content areas, centering around applying these skills in new situations
