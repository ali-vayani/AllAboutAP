About The Course

AP Spanish Language and Culture is a college-level course that is based on the Spanish language, as well as its influence on the Hispanic culture, communities, traditions, and families. Many of the students taking it will have already had some Spanish-speaking background, which is taken into account in the 80% pass rate across the last few years. If you do not have a Spanish-speaking background, expect a rigorous amount of coursework and studying if you are looking to succeed in the exam.

About The Exam

• Involves interpretive communication: print materials.

• Section 1B: MCQ with Audio: 55 minutes | 35 questions | 27% of total scores

• Involves interpretive communication: audio texts, and audio combined with print texts

• Free Response:

• Section 2A: (Written) 1 hour and 10 minutes | 2 questions | 25% of total score

• Interpersonal Writing: read and reply to an email message (15 minutes)

• Presentational Writing: write an argumentative essay (55 minutes)

• Section 2B: (Spoken) 18 minutes | 2 questions | 25% of total score

• Interpersonal Speaking: participate in 5 exchanges in a simulated conversation (20 seconds for each)

• Presentational Speaking: deliver a presentation (2 minutes long) over cultural comparison
