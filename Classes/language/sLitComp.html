About The Course

AP Spanish Literature and Culture is a college-level course that is based primarily on analyzing and comparing literary works from the Spanish-speaking world, as well as studying its influence on the Hispanic culture across a variety of texts, poems, and artworks. If you do not already have a Spanish-speaking background, expect a rigorous amount of coursework and studying if you are looking to succeed in the exam.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Monday, May 1st 12:00 PM

• Multiple Choice

• Section 1A: 20 minutes | 15 questions | 10% of total score

•Includes 3 sets of questions based on authentic audio texts including:

• An excerpt from an interview with an author

• A recited poem (not on the required reading list)

• A presentation on a literary topic related to course content

• Section 1B: 1 hour | 50 questions | 40% of total score

• Includes 6 sets of 7-10 questions based on readings from both in and out of the required reading list

• Free Response: 1 hour and 40 minutes | 4 questions | 50% of total score

• Section 2 Short Answer Questions (Suggested time: ~30 mins | 15 mins each)

• Students explain how the given theme in an excerpt relates to the literary work from which it was taken

• Students compare how a particular theme is represented in a given text and image

• 2 Essay Questions (Suggested time: ~70 minutes | 35 minutes each)

• Students analyze how a given text represents the characteristics of a certain genre as well as a particular historical, cultural, or social context.

• Students read 2 excerpts (one from a text on the required list, the other from a text not on the list) and analyze the effect of literary devices that the authors use in the texts to develop a particular theme that is provided in the question prompt.
