About The Course

In this class you’ll learn how to understand writing and rhetorical arguments through reading, analyzing, and writing texts. You’ll explore topics such as rhetorical situations, claims and evidence, reasoning and organization, and style.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Tuesday, May 9th 8:00 AM

• Multiple Choice: 45 Questions | 1 hour | 45% of the total score

• 23-25 Reading questions

• 20-22 Writing questions

• Free Response: 3 Questions | 2 hours and 15 mins | 55% of the total score

• Synthesis Question (6 pt)

• You’re provided with 6 - 7 texts about a specific topic. You’re asked to create an argument that combines and cites at least 3 of the sources to support your thesis

• Rhetorical Analysis (6 pt)

• You’ll read a nonfiction text and analyze the writes language choices and their intended meaning to the text.

• Argument (6 pt)

• You’ll be asked to create an evidence-based argument based on a given topic.

Resources

• Study Tips

• Read and analyze a variety of texts regularly. This will help you build your analytical skills and develop your understanding of different types of writing.

• Practice writing essays frequently. This will help you hone your writing skills and get used to the types of essay prompts that you may encounter on the exam.
