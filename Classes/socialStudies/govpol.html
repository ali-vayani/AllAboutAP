About The Course

AP US Government and Politics is an introductory college-level course in the government and politics of the United States. Students gain a better understanding of the political concepts covered in the course units, including your ability to analyze the foundational documents and to apply Supreme Court decisions you studied in the course to real-life scenarios.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Monday, May 1st 8:00 AM

• Multiple Choice: 55 Questions | 1 hour and 20 minutes | 50% of the total score

• The Multiple Choice will include individual, single, and sets of questions

• The questions will be based on:

• Supreme Court decisions in real-life scenarios

• Data in graphs, maps, charts, tables, etc.

• Comparing political concepts and processes

• Analyzing foundational documents and visual sources

• Free Response: 4 Questions | 1 hour and 40 mins | 50% of the total score

• 1 Concept Application Question

• Students will describe and compare political concepts

• 1 Quantitative Analysis Question

• Students will analyze quantitative data and explain how it relates to a political concept

• 1 SCOTUS Comparison Question

• Students will analyze, describe, and compare Supreme Court cases

• 1 Argument Essay

• Students will develop an argument to construct an essay while using evidence from foundational documents to answer the prompt
