About The Course

AP Comparative Government and Politics is an introductory college-level course in comparative politics. Students gain a better understanding of comparative government and politics by analyzing data and text-based sources, while also addressing several topics such as leadership and authority, stability, democratization, and types of political analysis.

About The Exam

• Exam Date: Wednesday, May 3rd 12:00 PM

• Multiple Choice: 55 Questions | 1 hour | 50% of the total score

• 40-44 of the Multiple Choice will have no stimulus material

• Quantitative Analysis (3 sets)

• Students will analyze and answer from a quantitative stimulus

• Qualitative Analysis (2 sets)

• Students will analyze and answer from text-based secondary sources

• The 6 countries addressed in the exam:

• China, Iran, Mexico, Nigeria, Russia, and the United Kingdom

• Free Response: 4 Questions | 1 hour and 30 mins | 50% of the total score

• Concept Application

• Students will describe and compare political concepts

• Quantitative Analysis

• Students will analyze quantitative data and explain how it relates to a political concept

• Comparative Analysis

• Students will compare political concepts among different countries

• Argument Essay

• Students will develop an argument to construct an essay while using evidence from listed countries to answer the prompt

Resources

• Study Tips

• Review all of the important topics that are covered in the course. You should use a combination of resources to review the topics, such as textbooks, review books, online resources, videos, flashcards

• Know the content and skills that are covered in the course and on the exam. The course covers six main units: introduction to comparative politics, sovereignty, authority, and power, political institutions, political and economic changes, citizen-state relations, and political challenges.
